The dataset was first made public on 1 August 2017 and included incidents from 1 April of that year onwards. It currently contains data through June 2018.

When the data was released, many news outlets raised questions as to why black citizens were the subject of 36% of the incidents even though they only make up 13% of the city's population.

Purpose of This Site

Both the US and the UK have recently seen large-scale protests in response to incidents of police brutality, particularly involving black citizens as the targets. This has led to a (often heated) dialogue concerning whether modern policing is systematically prejudiced against minorities. After the first round of the London Met database was published, it was widely reported that a disproportionate amount of these use of force incidents involved black citizens.

The purpose of the analysis performed for this site was to use machine learning techniques to determine what factors have the biggest impact on determining the outcome of these incidents. In other words, what is happening in some of these incidents but not others that is leading to either the victim or officer getting injured?

The details of this analysis are available to explore below. There are results of the initial analysis and an interactive interface that includes a map of the incident outcomes by borough as well as a map of the qualities of the boroughs that influence the incidents outcomes. Finally, there is an incident generator which allows the user to put in hypothetical characteristics of an encounter and the machine learning model created will give the user the chance of the subject and the officer getting injured if that incident were to occur in each of the boroughs.

The FBI are currently working on releasing a similar database for various US cities. When available, this data can be analyzed similarly. This will allow for an interesting comparison to be made between these incidents in a country where the police commonly carry firearms and one where they don’t.

Exploratory Analysis

As expected, subject's behavior is the most important factor with other tactics being tried before resorting to force.

However location and borough were in the top 10, suggesting where the incident takes place is important.

Factors relating to both the "percieved neighborhood quality" (subjective well-being score) and the "actual neighborhood quality" (borough unemployment rate and arson crimes) scored in the top 10 as well.

Hypothesis:The racial skew in targets is not the result of direct racism but possible profiling by neighborhood.
